The Lean Start-up

Eric Ries, an entrepreneur and author, writes about The Lean Start-up philosophy that start-up companies can use to successful build and launch a new products and services. His four principles of a lean start-ups provide a "start-up" framework for library systems and operations that can transform the services and technology we offer our patrons and improve the library's relevancy in a community or an organization. FRBR, RDA, & Redis MARCR Datastore

In our current library ILS, OPACS, and even next-generation of library services platforms being developed by library vendors, offer minimal support for FRBR and RDA. RDA, an effort lead by the ALA and others, is based on FRBR and is intended to be the replacement of AACR2. There is much concern about the added cost, lack of supporting technologies, and complexity for implementing RDA from the MARC21-based technologies being used and developed in libraries.

MARCR is the name given to a new proposal by the Library of Congress's Bibliographic Framework Initiative that borrows many concepts from both RDA and MARC21 to support a more of a Linked-Data approach to bibliographic records.
