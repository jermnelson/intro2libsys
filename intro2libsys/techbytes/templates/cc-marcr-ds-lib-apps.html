NoSQL & Cloud Computing

NoSQL is a general term for the class of newer data storage technologies that differ from traditional databases that use SQL. NoSQL is a very broad category and includes different types of technology to meet very different data storage, access, and scalability requirements.

NoSQL technology range from key-value datastores like Redis, to Bigtable inspired projects like Hadoop and Facebook's Cassandra, to document-oriented datastores like MongoDB, CouchDB and Amazon's SimpleDB.

Most of these technologies are used in either public, private, or hybrid clouds directly, or as core components of many cloud-based apps and services.
