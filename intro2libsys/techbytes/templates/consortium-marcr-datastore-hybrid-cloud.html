Evolving towards a Consortium MARCR Datastore in a Hybrid-Cloud

Colorado College is collaborating closely with the Colorado Alliance of Research Libraries on a multi-year project for a "union catalog" representing all of the Works in the Alliance's member institution while offering real-time metrics for the resource sharing of the member institution's Instances.

{% endblock %} {% block support-content %}

Local control that leverages the consortium's cloud

The Library consortium cloud treats each institution's MARC Instance, Annotation, and Authority Redis datastores as read-only. These individual Redis datastores are combined into a single read-only Redis search environment support by the consortium's master MARCR datastores.

Task Level Advantages

  • Bibliographic record workflow apps can easily be created for common tasks
  • Cataloging staff can focus on original cataloging and easily use the consortium's Work entities while describing the unique attributes of the library's instances.

Managerial Level Advantages

  • MARCR Datastore makes operational reporting much easier, can track activity of an institution's Instances in real-time or historical usage patterns.
  • New task-specific workflows that are good candidates for automation or for single-use specialized app can be developed with short delivery times ranging from weeks to months

Leadership Level Advantages

  • Reduce dependencies on expensive and complex commercial systems
  • Flexibility of the MARCR metadata Datastore offers new opportunities for library services in big data curation, digital humanities, and distance educational efforts such as Massive Open Online Courses (MOOC).
