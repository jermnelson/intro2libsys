    Introduction to Library Systems

    Group Project

    You and two or three of your classmates will put Agile development principles in practice as you create a small number of Access and Discovery HTML5 apps for a small collection of material owned by an external party. Many religious, civic, and other non-profit organizations have small collections of books, periodicals, video, or audio material. Corporations and small business often have small collections of industry or other material that exists as a disorganized lending collection.

    You group must identify and approach an organization and offer to catalog and implement at least three HTML5 apps to assist the collection's users in Accessing and Discovering the collection's material.

    Example Project

    To give you an idea on what is expected of your group's project; Jeremy Nelson has created three apps for a real Aikido dojo lending collection of Aikido DVDs. As the course progresses, the Pikes Peak Aikido Library will be used to demostrate and provide an example of modifing, extending, and creating new HTML5 apps with a Redis bibliographic datastore running on a Rackspace Linux Virtual Machine.

    Evaluation

    Your Group Project evaluated includes peer review of your participation and contribution, a self-evaluation, and evaluation by the organization whose collection you used for your project. More details on how your project is assessed, is available at http://www.intro2libsys.info/assessment/project.
