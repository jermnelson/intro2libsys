Colorado College

Big Idea Pitch

Software support and consulting start-up for a Catalog Pull Platform based upon research being done at CC and for the Library of Congress.

{# END Main Visual row #}

The Opportunity

Jeremy Nelson, Colorado College's Metadata and System Librarian, research and software development on library linked data and the semantic web was a critical reason for the Library of Congress awarding him and a library user-interface design firm Influx Design a contract for a new search and display catalog based on their new BIBFRAME linked-data vocabulary.

The library enterprise software market is ripe for disruption for a new platform that better leverages open-source cloud-computing technology for significant cost savings for libraries and cultural heritage organizations.

Why?

Libraries, Museums, & other Cultural Heritage Institutions need better catalogs for managing and curating existing and new modes of creative and scholarly expressions, data sets both large and small, for use by people now and future generations.
